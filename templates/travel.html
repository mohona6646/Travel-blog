Travel tips



Before exploring the unknown and visiting new places you have never seen, it is important to pack all the essentials and be prepared for any situation possible. I cannot stress this enough how important it is to pack everything you think you might need, from a light jacket to a first aid kit, do not neglect the importance of being prepared. In the end, it is always better to be more safe than sorry. I have learned this the hard way. In this page, I will tell you what to pack, how to be prepared and how to save your pockets as much as you can while having the time of your life!!



What to pack before traveling?

First and foremost, do not forget your passport!! As silly as this may seem, it happens to the best of us, so you really wanna make you have your passport before boarding the plane, and also at ALL TIMES. As for the essentials, do your research. It is important to be aware of the weather in the country and be prepared accordingly. Also, pro-tip, even if the country seems to warm at all times, bring a sweater or a light a jacket. It can get chilly at times, so definitely bring something to cover yourself up. Bring a few pair of undergarments and socks so you can change at anytime.


Another pro-tip is to plan your outfits ahead. Think of what you will be wearing everyday for the duration of your trip, and it doesn't hurt to repeat some outfits. I also want to bring your attention to running shoes! They are super important because you never know when you will be needing them. You might be having an excursion to the mountains or walking through the great forest. You definitely don't want to be wearing sandals. This should be all for your luggage. I also recommend people to bring a travel bag with them that they can carry anywhere. It is very useful and you can get have it on you at all times. You should keep your sanitary stuff in it and items from your first aid kit. Lastly, don't forget your camera! You would want to take good quality pictures of the scenery. This it it! Now, you are all ready to board the plane!



Tips to save your $

Who doesn't want to save their money? Traveling can be a little too expensive. With spending here and there on food, activities, accommodations, one can have too many debts. But fear not! With these tips, you can save a lot and not miss out on anything. My first financial advice would be to search up flight tickets in advance. There are tons of websites such as cheapflights.com that suggest tickets for not much. It is just a matter of good searching skills and being a couple weeks in advance. If you can, definitely plan the trip months advance. A pro tip is to not books when its the holidays. That's when most people decide to travel and the tickets get more expensive.


Another way to save money is by booking airbnbs with your friends instead of hotels. They can be way cheaper if you look for a good one and the price can be split among all friends which is even more convenient. For the Morocco trip, the cost was about 30$ per person for a night, which is definitely a steal.


My last tip to save your pockets is by commuting with cheaper transportation like the subway, if possible. This is a huge one because not everyone knows about this and they usually take ubers or taxis to get from one place to another when they can travel for way cheaper. That's we did for Japan. They also had bicycle stations that you rent for a couple of bucks for the day, which is even more fun. You get the view and the excitement from riding a bike on a different country. If you ask me, it's a bonus.



Be safe!!!



My last advice would be to be careful and prudent. You need that the food you are eating is actually safe and won't give you a tummy ache. This can be very annoying and in some extreme cases, painful as well. Especially when eating street food, it is important you ensure the safety of the food you are putting in system. Many people end up spending their journey on bed, trying to recover from food sickness. Another thing is to be careful when traveling alone. It can be dangerous, especially when you are a tourist. Some can scam you with a lot of things or you can get lost in the middle of nowhere. Also , get yourself a cellphone or some form of communication devices as they come really in handy. You don't need to pay expensive plans either. Just a few minutes of phone calls should more than enough. This is why it is important to be safe and always on the lookout for danger signs. I would say it is more safe to visit crowded areas rather than places that seem abandoned. Carry a map with you whenever you can. It can come really handy at times you would expect the least. And most importantly, do not hesitate to ask for help. The people there are usually very welcoming with open arms and are more than happy to help you.
